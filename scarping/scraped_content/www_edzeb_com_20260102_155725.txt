Exam 1 - Fundamental of Business Economics (BA 1)
Learn all about the economic and operating context of business and how the factors of competition, the behaviour of financial markets and government economic policy can influence an organisation.
Learning Objectives:
Macroeconomic and Institutional Context of Business
Microeconomic and Organisational Context of Business
Informational Context of Business
Financial Context of Business
Exam 2 - Fundamental of Management Accounting (BA 2)
Learn the core principles and essential concepts of management accounting and get insightful resources for mastering the fundamentals of financial decision-making and strategic planning.
Learning Objectives:
The Context of Management Accounting
Costing
Planning and Control
Decision Making
Exam 3 - Fundamental of Financial Accounting (BA 3)
In this subject, a learner can obtain a practical understanding of financial accounting and the process behind the preparation of financial statements for single entities.
Learning Objectives:
Accounting Principles, Concepts and Regulations
Recording Accounting Transactions
Preparation of Accounts for Single Entities
Analysis of Financial Statements
Exam 4 - Fundamentals of Ethics, Corporate Governance and Business Law (BA 4)
Dive into this subject to get insights and resources to navigate the dynamic landscape of responsible business practices and legal compliance.
Learning Objectives:
Business Ethics and Ethical Conflict
Corporate Governance, Controls and Corporate Social Responsibility
General Principles of the Legal System, Contract and Employment Law
Company Administration

Exam 1 - Managing Finance in a Digital World (E1)
Learn the key role that finance plays in an organisation, and how and why technologies and resources used to manage the finance functioning.
Learning Objectives:
Role of the finance function
Technology in a digital world
Data and information in a digital world
Shape and structure of the finance function
Finance interacting with the organisation
Exam 2 - Management Accounting (P1)
Know the techniques how budgets are prepared and implemented across the organisation to ensure sound short term decision making, against a backdrop of risk and uncertainty.
Learning Objectives:
Cost Accounting for decision and control
Budgeting and budgetary control
Short term commercial decision making
Risk and uncertainty in the short term
Exam 3 - Financial Reporting (F1)
Know all about financial reporting and tools, including how to distinguish between types of taxes, calculate corporation taxes and issues affecting taxation.
Learning Objectives:
Regulatory environment of financial reporting
Financial statements
Principles of taxation
Managing cash and working capital
Operational Case Study
At the Operational level, candidates simulate the role of an entry-level finance professional, using capstone case Study examination to showcase their acquired knowledge, techniques, skills, and mindset for this position.

Exam 1 - Managing Performance (E2)
Learn how different styles of leadership can be used to improve the performance of individuals to achieve organisational goals and how to use performance management concepts and techniques to implement effective strategies.
Learning Objectives:
Business models and value creation
Managing people performance
Managing projects
Exam 2 - Advanced Management Accounting (P2)
Learn how to use cost management, quality and process management and value management to provide organisations with cost advantage. Also, acquire the knowledge of the criteria, process and techniques that are used to decide which projects to undertake and how to analyse risks and uncertainties especially in capital investment.
Learning Objectives:
Managing the costs of creating value
Capital investment decision making
Managing and controlling the performance of organisational units
Risk and control
Exam 3 - Advanced Financial Reporting (F2)
Learn all about the sources and types of funds and how much they cost, including the key financial reporting standards, the International Integrated Reporting Framework and its components, and how to conduct analyses of financial statements and their limitations.
Learning Objectives:
Financing capital projects
Financial reporting standards
Group accounts
Integrated reporting
Analysing financial statements
Management Case Study
The role simulated is that of a finance manager in the Management level. This case study examination provides a simulated context which allows candidates to demonstrate the acquired knowledge, skills, techniques and the mindset required for that role.

Exam 1 - Strategic Management (E3)
Get in-depth knowledge of the foundation of strategic management and an understanding of the dynamics of the organisational ecosystem and how it affects the strategy of the organisation. Also, know how strategy is implemented, how implementation objectives are achieved and how change is managed.
Learning Objectives:
The Strategy process
Analysing the organisational ecosystem
Generating strategic options
Making strategic choices
Strategic control
Digital strategy
Exam 2 - Risk Management (P3)
Learn an effective way to identify, evaluate and manage enterprise risks. Know from where the strategic risks emanate from, how to evaluate them and understand how oversight of these risks is critical to the governance of the organisation and ways to manage them.
Learning Objectives:
Enterprise risk
Strategic risk
Internal controls
Cyber risk
Exam 3 - Financial Strategy (F3)
Dive into the subject to learn the different strategic financial objectives and get the details of where and how they access these funds at the right time, in the right quantities and at the right cost. Plus, know all about the sources of financial risk, how to evaluate and manage financial risk appropriately, and techniques used in business valuation.
Learning Objectives:
Financial policy decisions
Sources of long term funds
Financial risks
Business valuation
Strategic Case Study
The role that is simulated in the strategic level is of the senior finance manager. The Case Study Examination provides a simulated context which allows learners to attain the required knowledge, skills, techniques and mindset for that role.
